Basic Insight

Teen depression is a serious mental health problem that causes a persistent feeling of sadness and loss of interest in activities. It affects how your teenager thinks, feels and behaves, and it can cause emotional, functional and physical problems. Although depression can occur at any time in life, symptoms may be different between teens and adults.

Personality Characteristics That Are Susceptible to Depression

Depression can happen to anyone, but depression sufferers tend to be introverted, creative, or perfectionist. Personality does not cause depression but can be a risk factor.

  • General Anxiety: People with high levels of generalized anxiety are prone to depression because they tend to worry, exaggerate problems, and take everything personally or “take it personally.” Or in another sense, easily offended.
  • Shy/Covered: Individuals who tend to be quiet, often because they have been bullied or insulted, perceive social interactions as more threatening than being alone.
  • Narcissistic and Selfish: People who tend to be selfish, easily get angry at other people for no good reason, and often blame others when things don't go the way they think they should, can be more susceptible to depression.

Why Are Depression Sufferers Suffering from Oversleeping?

Getting enough sleep is also an important part maintaining a healthy body. However, sleeping too much can also be a sign of a mental disorder in the form of depression.

According to psychologist Michelle Drerup, not getting enough sleep or sleeping too much is a symptom of depression.

According to Drerup. 15 percent of people with depression experience excessive sleep, especially people with atypycal depression. Atypical depression is a type of depression in which the sufferer experiences mood that can improve in response to positive events.

The Effects of Depression Damage the Brain

Not only does it have a negative impact on psychological conditions or mental health, depression can also damage the sufferer's brain. A number of studies have found that people who experience depression are susceptible to premature aging of the brain and impaired brain function.

Symptoms of depression are different from ordinary feelings of sadness or sadness which will get better on their own. Without proper treatment, depression, which is a serious mental problem, can disrupt brain function and damage brain tissue.

Various causes of depression that damage the brain

Depression can be experioenced by anyone, wheter children, teenagers or adults. There are many factors that can cause depression, such as:

  • Traumatic life events, such as divorce or death.
  • Being a victim of violence or bullying.
  • Dependence on drugs and alcohol.
  • History of other mental disorders.
  • Disturbance in brain function, such as dementia or stroke.
  • Hereditary factors.

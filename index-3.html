Symptoms

Teen depression signs and symptoms include a change from the teenager's previous attitude and behavior that can cause significant distress and problems at school or home, in social activities, or in other areas of life. Depression symptoms can vary in severity, but changes in your teen's emotions and behavior may include examples like losing interest in things too quickly, constantly feeling down, etc.

Symptoms of Depression

Here are the key symptoms of depression:

  • Persistent Sadness or Low Mood: Pervasive feelings of helplessness, hopelessness, or depresion which prevail for more than half the days in the week.   • Changes in Appetite or Weight:Unintended changes in body weight, that is, the loss or gain of more than a few pounds, or conspicuous alternations in eating habits which include overeating or eating very sparingly.
  • Sleep Disturbances:Problems in getting to sleep, staying asleep (insomnia). or sleeping too mush (hypersomnia).
  • Fatigue or Low Energy:Inability to be active irrespective of the number of hours an individual has slept at night.
  • Feelings of Worthlessness or Guilt:Presistent thoughts and beliefes of low slef-esteem or guilt over trivial issues or past wrongdoings.
  • Difficulty Concentrating:Difficulty in focusing, recalling information, or making decisions.
  • Physical Symptoms: Headaches,digestive problems, or chronic pain which cannot be attribute to any certain physical conditions.
  • Suicidal Thoughts or Behaviours: Presence of ideas or acts related to death, dying, or suicide; in severe forms, obsessive thinking about suicide or actual planning for it.
  • irritability or Restlessness:Experiencing irritability, intolerance, or fidgeting without objective cause.

If one has more than five of these symptoms for over two weeks, they might be depressed and should seek medical attention.

Additional Information on Symptoms of Depression

Depression is not just about feeling sad; it can manifest in various physical, emotional, and cognitive symptoms. Some people may experience:

  • Social Withdrawal:Avoiding friends, family, and social activities, leading to isolation.
  • Physical Sluggishness or Agitation:Either slowing down physically (e.g., moving or speaking slowly) pr feeling restless and unable to sit still.
  • Negative Thinking:Having a pessimistic outlook on life, with thought such as "things will never get better."
  • Reduce LibidoA decrease in sexual desire or interest in intimacy
  • Substance Abuse:Turning to drugs, alcohol, or other substance to cope with feelings of depression.

It's important to note tha depression can vary greatly from person to person. If you or someone you know is experiencing these symptoms, it;s essential tp seek professional help.
